My first car ride in Canada

2012-03-22 14:56:04

Sygard

So, it is about -20 outside, and Im going all by myself to this hockey game where the Petes meet the Storm in CHL. The Petes where really putting up a fight for the first 40 minutes, also they had the best fighting scene Ive ever seen in a hockey game, nosebleed and everything. Unfortunately the Storm scores first, and then it is all over for the Petes, the game ends 3-0 closed by an open net goal. After the game I waited 20+ minutes for the buss. After finally getting to the transit station downtown where all the busses stop, I walk over to the line for the Trent University express bus. And then I meet Michelles. I ask her when the buss comes, and we stand and talk for like 30 minutes out in -20. Actually we really found the right tone, we decide to go in to the heat and wait, bus is still not coming, then she says some friends of her is going to drive her to the University and if I need a ride I can join, of course I do, we get safely back to the university, I shake Michelles hand, and is probably never going to see her again.

Great food great people!

2012-03-22 14:54:29

Sygard

After being friends with Min Sang, Hanseul and Kana for a while I got invited to a make your own countries food dinner. There were three girls from Japan, one girl from Thailand, and of course Hanseul and Min Sang from Korea. Since they looked much more suited for cooking than me and I was really hungry, I decided to not make any Norwegian food. One thing I learned that night was Asian people like to make sure they have enough food for everyone, so no one went home half full that night. There where all kinds of foods, the main course consisted of some REALLY spicy Korean soup with pepper that I urge you not to eat raw, the soup itself was one of the best soups Ive tasted. And the Japanese had some really tasty chicken breasts to serve, for desert Natasha from Thailand served delicious ginger tee with some kind of rice balls, I even got some of it to bring home!
